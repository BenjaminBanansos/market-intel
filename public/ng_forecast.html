What is EWMA Volatility?

JP Morgan's RiskMetrics model weights recent price moves more heavily than older ones using an exponential decay factor (λ = 0.94 for daily data). This means a sharp swing yesterday matters far more than a move three months ago.

For Natural Gas, EWMA volatility is critical because NG is one of the most volatile commodities — weather events, EIA storage reports, and LNG export surges can cause 5–15% daily moves. A rising EWMA line signals the market is entering a high-risk regime; falling means the market is calming.

Mean Reversion Forecast — Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Path

Why Commodities Mean-Revert

Unlike equities, Natural Gas prices are anchored by production economics. When NG spikes far above the historical mean (~$--/MMBtu), producers increase drilling and LNG imports rise — pushing price back down. When it crashes below cost-of-production, rigs shut in and supply shrinks — pushing price up.

This Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model estimates the speed and target of that reversion. A reversion speed of 5% per period means the price closes 5% of the gap between current price and the historical mean each period.
